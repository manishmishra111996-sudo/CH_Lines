1. Point
The most basic unit in geometry
Definition: A point marks a precise location in space. It has no length, breadth, or height. Denoted by a capital letter like A, B, P.

A point is just a dot. Think of it like the tip of a very sharp pencil pressing on paper — it shows WHERE, but has zero size.

Key Fact: Infinite points lie on any single line. Two distinct points always determine exactly one unique line.

2. Line Segment
Fixed length, two endpoints
Definition: A line segment is the shortest path between two endpoints. It has a definite, measurable length. Written as AB̄.
AB = BA in length. A segment has exactly one midpoint.
🌟 Real Life: Edge of a ruler, sides of your notebook!
〰️
3. Line
Definition: A line extends infinitely in both directions. No endpoints. Written as ↔AB.
🌟 Real Life: Horizon line, railway tracks extending to distance!
➡️
4. Ray
Definition: A ray starts from a fixed initial point and extends infinitely in one direction. Written as →AB.
⚠️ Ray AB ≠ Ray BA! Direction matters.
🌟 Real Life: Sunlight, laser pointer, torch beam!
📐
5. Angle — Definition & Formation
Definition: An angle is formed when two arms (rays) meet at a common vertex. Named as ∠AOB.

Angles are measured in degrees (°). Full rotation = 360°. Straight = 180°. Right = 90°.

7. Pairs of Angles
Complementary Angles: Sum = 90°. Example: 30° + 60° = 90° ✓
Supplementary Angles: Sum = 180°. Example: 110° + 70° = 180° ✓
Adjacent Angles: Share common vertex and one arm. No overlap.
Vertically Opposite Angles: When two lines intersect, opposite angles are always equal.
Linear Pair: Adjacent angles on a straight line. Sum always = 180°.
Formulae: Complement = 90° − θ  |  Supplement = 180° − θ
🛤️
8. Parallel & Perpendicular Lines + Transversal
Parallel Lines: Lines that never meet. Symbol: l ∥ m
Perpendicular Lines: Meet at exactly 90°. Symbol: l ⊥ m
Transversal: A line cutting across two or more other lines, forming angle pairs.
🌟 Real Life: Railway tracks = parallel lines. Ladder against tracks = transversal!

NCERT Solved Examples
Chapter 2 · Class 6 · All key solved questions
Example 1Angle types

Classify: (a) 45° (b) 115° (c) 90° (d) 180° (e) 75° (f) 215°

(a) 45° → Acute   (b) 115° → Obtuse   (c) 90° → Right
(d) 180° → Straight   (e) 75° → Acute   (f) 215° → Reflex
Example 2Complementary angles

Find complement of (a) 35° (b) 72° (c) 45°

Formula: Complement = 90° − angle
(a) 90°−35° = 55°   (b) 90°−72° = 18°   (c) 90°−45° = 45° ← its own complement!
Example 3Supplementary angles

Find supplement of (a) 100° (b) 55° (c) 90°

Formula: Supplement = 180° − angle
(a) 180°−100° = 80°   (b) 180°−55° = 125°   (c) 180°−90° = 90° ← right angle is its own supplement!
Example 4Vertically opposite angles

AB and CD intersect at O. ∠AOC = 50°. Find ∠BOD, ∠AOD, ∠BOC.

∠BOD = 50° (vertically opposite to ∠AOC)
∠AOD = 180°−50° = 130° (linear pair)
∠BOC = 130° (vertically opposite to ∠AOD)
Example 5Linear pair + algebra

POQ is straight line. Ray OR: ∠POR = 3x, ∠QOR = 2x. Find x.

3x + 2x = 180° (linear pair) → 5x = 180° → x = 36°
∠POR = 108°, ∠QOR = 72°. Check: 108°+72° = 180° ✓
